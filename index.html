Bacolod, Philippines

Bacolod, often called the "City of Smiles," is a vibrant and highly urbanized city located in the Negros Island Region of the Philippines. It serves as the capital of Negros Occidental and is known for its warm and friendly people, as well as its rich cultural heritage.

The city is famous for the annual MassKara Festival, a colorful celebration featuring street dancing, elaborate masks, and lively music. Bacolod is also a hub for the sugar industry, with its history deeply tied to sugarcane plantations. Visitors can explore landmarks like the San Sebastian Cathedral, the Bacolod Public Plaza, and the Capitol Park and Lagoon.

With a population of over 600,000 as of the 2020 census, Bacolod is a bustling city that offers a mix of modern amenities and traditional charm. It's a great destination for food lovers, offering local delicacies like chicken inasal and piaya.
