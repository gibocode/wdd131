About Think Pawsitive

Our Story

It all started with one happy tail.

Think Pawsitive was born from the belief that every dog deserves a life filled with love, kindness, and tail-wagging joy. What started as a personal journey with one anxious pup and a lot of trial-and-error training turned into a full-blown passion to help other dog parents build stronger, more positive bonds with their best friends.

We're here to make life better — for dogs and the humans who love them.

Our Mission

Spreading pawsitivity, one pup at a time.

At Think Pawsitive, our mission is to support dogs in living their best lives through positive reinforcement, holistic wellness, and plenty of play. We champion training methods based on trust, products that are safe and natural, and a lifestyle that celebrates the special connection we share with our canine companions.

What We Do

We help good dogs become great dogs — and keep tails wagging.

We offer:
  • Training resources rooted in positive reinforcement
  • Natural wellness and calming aids for pups of all ages
  • Dog-approved gear and enrichment tools
  • Feel-good stories, tips, and dog parenting advice
  • And a community that believes every dog is a good dog

The Pawsitive Philosophy

Train with kindness. Lead with love.

Dogs aren't just pets — they're family. That's why we focus on:
  • Patience-based, fear-free training
  • Confidence-building for dogs and humans
  • Enrichment and mental stimulation
  • Supporting rescue and shelter pups
  • Spreading good vibes through everything we do
We believe the best way to teach a dog is to understand them.

Meet the Pack

We're a team of dog lovers, trainers, and treat-givers.
Every product, post, and paw-sitive tip we share comes from a place of love and experience.

Paws in the Community

Dogs deserve the best — no matter their background.

We proudly support local rescues, shelters, and community outreach programs. A portion of every purchase goes toward helping shelter dogs find forever homes, and we regularly host donation drives and awareness campaigns for pups in need.
